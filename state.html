Deception Pass State Park

Deception Pass State Park, located in Washington, is renowned for its stunning landscapes and rich history. Spanning over 4,134 acres, the park encompasses diverse ecosystems, including old-growth forests, beaches, and lakes, making it a favorite for outdoor enthusiasts. It ranks as one of the most popular state parks in the U.S., attracting visitors with its iconic Deception Pass Bridge, which connects Whidbey Island and Fidalgo Island.

Wallace Falls State Park

Wallace Falls State Park, located in Snohomish County, Washington, spans 1,380 acres along the Wallace River. It features three stunning waterfalls, including the impressive 367-foot Wallace Falls, which cascades in three tiers. The park is characterized by old-growth forests, backcountry lakes, and remnants of its logging history. Visitors can enjoy 12 miles of hiking trails, perfect for all skill levels, as well as camping and picnicking facilities. Its natural beauty makes it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts year-round.

Larrabee State Park

Larrabee State Park, Washington's first designated state park, covers 2,748 acres along the scenic Chuckanut Drive. Established in 1915 through donations from Frances P. Larrabee and her family, the park features over 8,100 feet of saltwater shoreline on Samish Bay, as well as two freshwater lakes, Fragrance and Lost Lakes, known for excellent fishing. Visitors can enjoy a variety of activities including hiking, biking, boating, and beach exploration. The park is home to diverse ecosystems, with trails winding through lush forests and offering stunning views of the San Juan Islands. Larrabee is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts year-round.
